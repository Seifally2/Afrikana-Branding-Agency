HAVE QUESTIONS?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our UI/UX design services.

UI (User Interface) design focuses on the visual elements of a product - the look and feel, the presentation, and the interactivity of a product. UX (User Experience) design focuses on the overall experience of the user, including usability, accessibility, and the efficiency in accomplishing tasks. While UI is about how the product looks, UX is about how it works and feels.

The timeline varies depending on project complexity, but most UI/UX projects follow this general timeline: Discovery & Research (1-3 weeks), Information Architecture (1-2 weeks), Wireframing (2-3 weeks), UI Design (3-5 weeks), Prototyping & Testing (2-3 weeks). A complete redesign typically takes 8-16 weeks from start to finish.

Our standard deliverables include: User research reports, Personas & user journeys, Information architecture & sitemaps, Wireframes & prototypes, High-fidelity UI designs, Design systems & style guides, Interactive prototypes, Usability testing reports, and Developer handoff documentation. We tailor deliverables to each project's specific needs.

We measure UX success through both qualitative and quantitative metrics including: Task completion rates, Time on task, Error rates, User satisfaction scores (SUS, NPS), Conversion rates, Retention rates, Support ticket reduction, and Engagement metrics. We establish benchmarks before redesign and measure improvements post-launch.

Yes, we offer several post-launch support options: Design maintenance packages, UX analytics review sessions, Quarterly UX audits, A/B testing support, Feature enhancement design, and Design system updates. Many clients choose to retain us for ongoing optimization as user needs and technologies evolve.
